The story behind Summit

Built by a cyclist,
for cyclists who train seriously.

Summit didn't start as a startup idea. It started as a training problem — one that too many serious amateurs know by heart.

@projectsummit.app jordi@projectsummit.app

Jordi Espanyol

Founder · Project Summit

I'm an amateur cyclist and software developer based in Barcelona. Like many serious amateurs, I train consistently, race occasionally, and take my performance seriously — without the budget or time of a professional.

For years I managed my training across four or five different apps. Each one did its thing reasonably well. None of them talked to each other. At the end of every week I had data everywhere and clarity nowhere.

I knew what I had trained. I rarely knew what I should do next — or why.

Summit is my answer to that problem. Not a theoretical one, but the platform I wished existed when I was training for my first Gran Fondo and staring at a TSB chart I couldn't act on.

The problem · The solution

The problem

Too many apps. Too little clarity.

Serious amateurs juggle an average of three to five fitness apps simultaneously. Each captures part of the picture — activities, health metrics, nutrition, training load — but none synthesises it into a clear decision.

You end up knowing what happened. Not what to do.

The solution

One platform. Clear decisions.

Summit integrates training load, HRV-driven recovery, power analysis, and nutrition into a single adaptive engine. It doesn't just show you data — it tells you what to do tomorrow and why.

The plan rewrites itself around your life. Missed a session. Got sick. Travelled. Summit adjusts without you having to think about it.

Design principles

01

Data to decisions

Every metric in Summit exists to produce a recommendation. If a number doesn't change what you do, it shouldn't be on the screen.

02

Adapts to real life

Athletes get sick, travel, and miss sessions. The plan that doesn't account for this isn't a plan — it's a wishlist. Summit rewrites around reality.

03

Serious, not complicated

Professional-grade science shouldn't require a sports science degree to interpret. Summit speaks in training terms, not in jargon.
