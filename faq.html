The app

Where do the training plans come from? +

Summit's plans are generated by a proprietary periodisation engine — not by AI or a chatbot. The engine applies established sports science methodology (polarised training, CTL/ATL/TSB load management) and produces the same type of decisions a human coach would make: when to load, when to recover, when to taper.

The difference is that Summit runs these calculations continuously, in real time, and adapts them to your actual data — not to an idealised version of your schedule.

Is Summit an AI app? +

Depends on how you define AI. There's no large language model, no chatbot, and no generative content. Summit's engine is a deterministic algorithmic system: it follows codified sports science rules and produces predictable, explainable recommendations.

We think "algorithm" is more honest than "AI" here. Every decision Summit makes can be traced back to a specific rule or calculation — not a black box.

What happens when I miss a session? +

Summit detects it automatically and recalculates your plan. This is called plan cascading — skipped sessions, completed sessions, illness, travel, or any change in availability all trigger an automatic adjustment of what comes next.

You'll never be stuck following a plan that stopped making sense three days ago.

Can I record other types of training — gym, running, hiking? +

Yes. Activities from other disciplines are imported and contribute to your overall training load calculation. Summit accounts for the fatigue they generate.

That said, Summit doesn't structure or analyse non-cycling activities in detail. The training plan is built around cycling. Cross-training is acknowledged, not coached.

Devices & integrations

Do I need a power meter? +

For training load calculations, no. Summit uses heart rate and perceived exertion to estimate load when power data isn't available.

For structured workouts, yes for now. Workouts are currently prescribed in power zones (% FTP), so you need a power meter to follow them precisely. Roadmap Heart rate zone prescriptions are coming — this is a known priority.

Do I need an Apple Watch? +

No. Summit reads HRV, sleep, and recovery data directly from Apple Health. Any wearable that writes those metrics to Apple Health is compatible.

In practice, Apple Watch currently provides the best and most consistent data for Summit's recovery calculations. Other devices may write partial data depending on their Apple Health integration.

Does Summit work without a Strava account? +

Strava is currently the main way Summit imports completed activities. Without Strava, you'd need to log activities manually, which means you'd lose the automatic plan adaptation.

Direct integrations with additional platforms are on the roadmap. For now, Strava is strongly recommended for the full experience.

How do workouts get to my Garmin or Zwift? +

Summit pushes structured workouts to your devices via intervals.icu, which acts as a distribution layer. From there, workouts sync automatically to Garmin Connect, Zwift, and MyWhoosh.

You connect intervals.icu once in Summit's settings — after that it's automatic.

Can I import my existing Strava history? +

Yes. When you connect Strava, Summit imports your recent activity history automatically. This gives the engine real data to work with from day one — your eFTP estimation and load baselines are calculated from your actual rides, not from scratch.

Platform & pricing

Is Summit available on Android? +

Not yet. Summit is iOS-first. Android is on the roadmap for 2027, but iOS is the focus for the initial launch.

If you're on Android, join the waitlist anyway — Android users will be the first to know when it's ready.

Does it work for running or swimming? +

Not in v1.0. Summit is built exclusively for cycling in its first release. Running and swimming support are planned for 2027 — the training engine will expand to multi-sport once the cycling platform is solid.

How much does Summit cost? +

Pricing hasn't been announced yet. Summit Premium will have two tiers: Pro (detailed training analysis) and Elite (full platform access including advanced nutrition, pacing, and priority support).

Waitlist members will get priority access and will be among the first to know pricing when it goes live.

When does Summit launch? +

Summer 2026 on the App Store. The app is currently in closed beta with a group of cyclists helping test and refine it.

Join the waitlist to get early access when doors open.

Can I export my data? +

Yes. Your data belongs to you. You can request an export of your training data at any time, and you can delete your account — and all associated data — directly from within the app.
